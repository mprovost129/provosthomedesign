About Michael Provost

A business owner building for other business owners.

I build websites from both sides of the desk: as the person responsible for the code and as the person who depends on the finished system to support real work.

I am Michael Provost. I built the platform behind Provost Home Design because the business needed more than a brochure site. It needed plan discovery, structured inquiries, customer tools, administration, and room to grow.

That experience shapes every web engagement. A site has to make sense to a first-time visitor, but it also has to remain useful to the people answering leads, updating content, reviewing data, and paying the ongoing bills.

I also built the website for J. Fisk Construction, translating a local contractor's work into a focused online presence. The web practice now leads with clear business websites and takes on custom Django applications when the operational need justifies a deeper build.

The owner-operator perspective

The website is part of the business, not a separate art project.

Design decisions are evaluated against customer understanding, day-to-day operation, and long-term responsibility.

01 / CUSTOMER

Make the path clear.

Visitors should quickly understand the offer, the evidence, and the next useful action.

02 / OPERATOR

Respect the handoff.

Access, administration, maintenance, recurring costs, and ownership need to remain understandable after launch.

03 / INVESTMENT

Build only what earns its place.

Custom code is valuable when it solves an operational problem, not when a simpler site would do the job better.
